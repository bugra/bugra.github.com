Book Review: Visualizing Information for Advocacy

Introduction

I just finished reading the Visualizing Information for Advocacy.(Full disclosure: I received the copy to write this review) The book is about information visualization, particularly in advocacy and different campaigns as its name suggests.
It does this by going through more than 50 different (successful) campaigns and visualizations accompanying with them. Analysis of campaigns are useful and beneficial to convince the reader and furher back their arguments for visualization. However, the book's structure is not limited to the campaign analysis and strong examples as they want to also introduce powerful visualization concepts as well. Therefore, it could be also considered as a visualization book as well.

Things I liked about the book

  • Every subsection is coherent, consistent and tells its story quite well, supported by examples and other campaigns. Maybe, this one is not very surprising as the book is mainly about communicating ideas through visualizations for social good.
  • Colors, design and visualizations are well-selected and overall the book layout becomes very nice and it makes reading experience a joy.
  • The visualizations or the campaigns are kept separately from the story. Whenever they are needed, they are introduced in the section. This prevents book becoming an analysis of the successful campaigns book.
  • Examples and campaigns come from different domains(political, environment, war) which enriches the ideas.
  • Generally, the ideas are written in list and subheaders are used as much as possible. Another organization goodness.
  • As far as I could see, there is little to none repetition even though some of the sections have (naturally) overlapping parts. The repetition is kept minimal.
  • You just not learn visualization or information visualization but also a lot of problems that people have throughout the world. Remember the title also includes "advocacy". Your awareness of the some problems increase after reading the book.

Things that I did not like about the book

  • I wish the whole framework that the book tries to explain should be presented to the reader earlier not on the pg 124. This not only gives a bigger picture to reader but also presents the sections in an early stage so that reader could easily look at the this framework as he reads the chapters.
  • It is short book after all. I wanted more analysis and background information for the campaigns. As most of the visualizations are highly social or political, they are generally more interesting to people who somehow affected. An example is that Making the Visible Invisible campaign is not well explained, so even the visual elements are there, since the story of the campaign is lacking, understanding become incomplete. Explaining the problem and how the campaign tries to advocate for this problem is as important as the analysis of the campaign to the reader.
  • Some of the sections are not well connected to each other. The sections separately are coherent small pieces. However, some of the connections between different sections are not well thought.
  • The links that are in the footnotes are not shortened in any form and it is not fun to type whole url in your browser.

Book talks about three main approaches for advocacy:

  1. Get the Idea
  2. Get the Picture
  3. Get the Detail

Get the Idea

  • Use to Expose
  • Works by Evoking Reactions
  • Common Formats: Simple Graphics and images, posters, simple infographics
  • Approaches: Juxtapose, Subvert, Invert, Materialize, Compare, Contrast, Illuminate, Provoke, Parody, Intrigue
  • Interplay: The visual is the leading factor, often using representation; information is in the background and technology is mainly used for publication and promotion.

Get the Picture

  • Use to Explain
  • Works by Tellling stories
  • Common Formats: Infographics, animations, maps
  • Approaches: Associations, Connections, Remedies, Correlations, Portraits, Perceptions, Interactions
  • Interplay: Balance of the visual and of information, technology can be used as an essential aspect of this technique, or it may be important for publication and promotion

Get the Detail

  • Use to Explore
  • Works by Building Journeys
  • Common Formats: Dynamic databases and complex visualizations, interactive infographics
  • Approaches: Making data meaningful, putting together the facts, filling in the gaps, unravelling connections, connecting the dots, piecing together the facts, uncovering tracks, working from the ground up.
  • Interplay: Information is critically important; the visual aspect serves as organising principle and entry point; technology usually plays a strong role.
