## What is it? ### Recommender Systems - Collaborative filtering is a method of making automatic predictions (filtering) about the interests of a user by collecting preferences or taste information from many users (collaborating) > If most of the users buy an item with another item in Amazon, then that item is likely to be purchased with another item by another user.
### CB Insights - We have a lot of deals of investors(actually that is how we make money!) - Why not use the investors deal history to recommend which companies are likely to invest. - Reverse problem holds true. Which company should contact which investor to raise funding ? > Raising funding is one of the most enjoyable thing in building a company said no one ever.
### Use Case - When RRE VC came in, what did he say? He wants to know which company is more interest to him? - He gave an exmple of [Digit](https://digit.co/). If we have this company in the database, radar of other similar investors, we could recommend it to RRE even if we have no information about the company.
#### Implicit Feedback - We could store the company views of VC and then use this __implicit__ feedback mechanism to recommend more companies. - Right now, tools are more focused on the heuristics and keyword search on the company description. - But we could do fairly good with deal search history, but also with this implicit feedback mechanism. - [Spotify uses implicit feedback to recommend songs](http://stanford.edu/~rezab/nips2014workshop/submits/logmat.pdf)
